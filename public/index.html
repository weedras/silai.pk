About Us — Silai.pk

Every stitch has a story. Ours begins with a mother. Her name is Naik Bibi.

As a young girl in Pattoki, she learned the art of stitching from her brother — sitting beside him, watching his hands, falling in love with thread and fabric before she even had words for it.

That childhood passion never left her. Years later, after marriage, she turned that love into discipline — enrolling in a tailoring diploma at a local vocational institute and graduating with top marks. Then she did what every great craftsperson eventually does: she started teaching. She opened her own academy in her hometown, where young girls came to learn not just how to stitch, but how to carry themselves with the quiet confidence of someone who has mastered something real.

Life eventually asked her to pause. Weak eyesight, growing children, a home to run. The academy closed. But the passion never died.

Years later, a phone call changed everything.

A dear friend, an overseas Pakistani living in Canada, said something simple: "Finding a good tailor here is nearly impossible — and when you do, it costs a fortune."

We knew that feeling. Millions of Pakistani women abroad know it — beautiful unstitched fabric sitting in a suitcase for years, because no one nearby can cut a gala the way home could.

So we built the bridge.

Silai.pk is a platform for overseas Pakistanis. Send us your fabric, or let us source the finest unstitched cloth from top Pakistani brands — and we'll stitch it with the same love, care, and mastery Naik Bibi always poured into her work. Then we deliver it straight to your doorstep, wherever in the world you call home.

We are not just a tailoring service. We are the feeling of opening a parcel and finding that something finally fits — in every sense of the word.

Stitched in Pakistan. Delivered to your world.

Return & Refund Policy

Custom Tailoring Policy

Since every garment at Silai.pk is custom-stitched based on your specific requirements and measurements, we follow a strict No Return, No Refund, and No Cancellation policy.

1. Finality of Orders

Once an order is confirmed and the fabric has been cut, it cannot be cancelled or refunded. We encourage all customers to participate in the initial WhatsApp consultation to ensure every detail is correct before we begin.

2. Alterations

While we do not offer refunds, we are committed to your satisfaction. If there is a minor fit issue, we offer a one-time free alteration service. The customer will be responsible for shipping costs to our workshop.

3. Damaged Items

In the rare event that you receive a garment with a major manufacturing defect (not related to measurements), please contact us within 48 hours of delivery for a case-by-case review.

Shipping & Customs

Global Delivery

We ship worldwide from our Lahore workshop via express couriers (DHL, FedEx, UPS) to ensure your garments reach you safely and quickly.

1. Shipping Timelines

  • Standard Stitching: 10-14 business days + 3-5 days shipping.
  • Express Stitching: 4-7 business days + 3-5 days shipping.

2. Customs & Duties

International shipments may be subject to import duties and taxes once they reach your country. These charges are the responsibility of the recipient. Silai.pk has no control over these charges and cannot predict what they may be.

3. Tracking

Once your order is shipped, you will receive a tracking number via WhatsApp and Email. You can also track your progress in real-time on our Tracking Page.

📏 Measurement Guide

How to measure yourself at home — in 6 easy steps.

Measurements in inches

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • Use a soft measuring tape
  • Measure over your undergarments, not over clothes
  • Keep the tape snug but not tight
  • Ask a friend to help for accuracy
  • Still unsure? WhatsApp us a photo of a well-fitting outfit
①

Chest (Seena)

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape horizontal. Note this in inches.

②

Waist (Kamar)

Measure around the narrowest part of your natural waist — usually about 1 inch above your belly button.

③

Hips (Kamar/Koolha)

Measure around the fullest part of your hips and buttocks, about 8 inches below the waist.

④

Shoulder Width (Kandha)

Measure from the edge of one shoulder to the other, across the back. This determines the kameez width at the top.

⑤

Kameez Length (Lambai)

From the top of your shoulder down to where you want the kameez to end. Typical: 42–50 inches depending on style.

⑥

Trouser / Shalwar Length

From your natural waist down to your ankle. Usually 38–42 inches. For Tulip Shalwar, measure to just below the knee.

✂

Sleeve Length (Aastaeen)

From the shoulder point down to the wrist. For full sleeves: ~21–23 inches. For 3/4 sleeves: ~16–18 inches.
